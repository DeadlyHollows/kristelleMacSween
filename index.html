  • Canadian Wildlife Federation / CMARA
  • Role: Designer
  • March 2016
  • Development: Undergoing

Contributions: High Fidelity Mockups of Home Screens

Overview:

The Canadian Marine Animal Response Aliance (CMARA) were looking for a Web redesign. Their current design was too dark and boxy. They were hoping for something lighter, in blues, with more visuals and better attention to typography. It was important to them that the "Report An Emergency Here" and "Donate" buttons be big and bright. They suggested using a life preserver.

I decided to play with the shape of the CMARA logo in the hero image, to give the image layers that would fit the water theme and compliment the shape of the whale. I drew some new big bright "Report An Emergency Here" and "Donate" buttons. They really can't be missed now. I added a dark blue gradient to the photos, to add harmony. I also added a new typeface to the design to help give the text better contrast, visual hierarchy and appeal. I added a few embelishments to make it pop.

I designed custom mobile-compatible screens to ensure that it is beautiful on all devices.

  • Presentey Engineering Products Limited
  • Role: Lead Designer
  • January 2016 - March 2016
  • Development - Undergoing

Contributions: Rebranding, Web Style Guide, Wireframes, High Fidelity Mockups, Photo editing, poster design, and assisted with the Scope Document.

Overview:

The main objective of this project was to design and develop a prototype for the currently outdated Presentey Engineering Products Limited(PEPL) website, that would serve to improve visual presentation, usability, performance, and website traffic. Our goal was to provide users with a better understanding of who they are, what they have to offer and what sets them apart. A modern design that is technologically current would better represent and reflect PEPL’s reputation for excellence and their dedication to providing their clients with innovative custom solutions.

One of our primary goals was to highlight their very unique products and services in a way that is more accessible, aesthetically beautiful and informative. We designed the website to be mobile compatible and optimized for fast web viewing. We also improved the user experience by minifying the site, adding a contact form and providing easy navigation for users to quickly identify appropriate solutions and submit technical inquiries. We aimed to leave users with a world class impression that supports PEPL’s reputation for excellence.

Presentey Engineering were hoping for a new logo concept. I designed several concepts for them to chose between, as a temporary placeholder for the Web Revamp. They were so pleased with my concepts, they decided to keep one as their new logo. I used Google Fonts to ensure that it would be optimized and free to use! The final logo was handed off to them in both web and print formats. Additionally, they were provided with a favicon and Web Style Guide.

Some Notable Improvements

We decided to minify the site and add a search bar and contact form to improve the usability. The user can now quickly identify and locate what they need, as well as make inquiries.

The Presentey Engineering website features local weather conditions calibrated by a modular meteorological station which measures factors such as wind speed & direction, temperature, humidity, and pressure. We decided to place the details in a stylish pop-up, to avoid the user being required to hit refresh for real time weather conditions.

Challenges We Conquered

One of the challenges we faced was their outdated method of displaying products as PDFs. Our team spent time photographing numerous products to display in organized product sliders. The new product sliders and images are much more visually appealing, modern and user friendly. We believe this to be an improvement that is well worth the effort and time consuming transition.

Twinkle Toes - Project

The task was to invent and brand a product, then make a motion graphics ad to market the it. With so much creative freedom, I decided to use my niece's toes as inspiration. I used Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop and After Effects.

I wanted to design a logo that was whimsical and sweet. Something that would work well on the product, and appeal to the target market. I created a simple wordmark, as well as a more elaborate version that could be animated, and used on the product bottles which come in various colours. I made it in white and in purple!

I had so much fun creating the graphics and playing with colours that I love.

This was my final submission. Not bad for my first ad!
